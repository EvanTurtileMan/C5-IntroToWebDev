C5 - Introduction to Web Development
Welcome to the C5 Tutorial Class: Introduction to Web Development!
In this tutorial class, you will learn how to develop some simple applications on the web. Whenever we surf the web, we are constantly accessing and using these applications. For example, we use Google to search up our items of interest or we go to Youtube to watch interesting videos uploaded by many users. Sometimes, we play games on our browsers! The list goes on. As you might expect, the contents of this course will demonstrate how one may go about creating such an application and how to deploy it into the real world.
When you join this class, you will be creating simple web applications using Node.js. We will be covering the essentials first before we go into tougher concepts such as backend creation and security within the Node.js application. Be mindful that although this is a course where you apply your knowledge in projects and labs, you will be learning the concepts and theories as well.

Course Information
Welcome to the C5 tutorial class: Introduction to Web Development! In this intermediate-leveled tutorial class, you will learn how to develop simple web applications through the use of Node.js. During the 10 weeks in this tutorial course, you will learn the essentials of web development, from how to design a webpage through HTML and CSS to how to develop the backend of your Node.js web application. Of course, although you learn the theory of how to develop such web applications, you will also apply your knowledge and practice how to create web applications.

In this tutorial class, there will be two projects. These projects help you apply the knowledge and theory you have learned throughout this tutorial class. The first project will be based on lessons and labs of Weeks 1 to 3 (it is mainly based on frontend design) while the second project will be based on lessons and labs of Weeks 4 to 10 (which includes backend implementation, but it will be built up on top of Weeks 1 to 3, which is given). Please note that these two projects have to be very different from each other; you cannot build on from your first project and then pass it off as your second project. Each project should help you apply your knowledge on frontend design and backend implementation and allow you to practice and understand even more. You may recycle SOME ideas from your first project and carry it over to your second project, but you cannot build on top of your first project. Of course, you cannot upload your lab work and call it off as your second project; otherwise, you will receive a mark of 0. These two projects will be due a week after the course-end test (which is in April 2018). The course instructor will give you a few ideas that you can work with for both projects in-class. Just like the labs, you must use Github to upload your work. On the second project, you may work in groups of 2 or 3, and never alone. However, on the first project, you MUST work by yourself as it is just frontend design.
In this tutorial class, there will be a course-end test you will have to write. It will account a hefty 30% of your final mark in this tutorial class. It will cover all topics taught in this tutorial class. You will be given 120 minutes to write this test; you are allowed to write your own cheat sheet (8 x 11 inches, double sided) and you may bring it to the test.
In this tutorial class, you MUST use Github to be able to hand in your work. What is Github? It is a service that allows you to post your work in a "folder" called a repository (which is unique to the work you post into it). You can allow other collaborators to join (those you want to work with) a particular repository, and you can share work with many others. Github acts like a portfolio of sorts for us Computer Scientists and Software/Computer/Electrical/(insert engineering title here) engineers, sort of like how art students save their art work in a portfolio folder. You should only upload your work on Github; do NOT send me an email containing a zip folder of your work and do not give me any Dropbox links containing your work. Do not scp your work to me as well... I will only check Github and mark what is inside the repository I have shared with you/the repository you have shared with me.
In this tutorial class, we will be using "course_PORTaL", an application that was created from bottom-up by yours truly, just for your convenience! This web application details all of the courses you have joined in the past and will show your current final mark based on the work you have handed in and what has been marked. It will be also a place to share Github links with me if you have created your own repositories and would like to hand in those repositories instead. With each work you hand in, everything marked will be shown on course_PORTaL. The instructor will create an account for you; you will receive your username and password through text/email. You can change your password through this application.
